Center for Street Children

In 1991, the Educational Research and Development Assistance (ERDA) Foundation, Incorporated came up with a special project for streetchildren, the Tuklas (discovery), Kalinga (care), Laruan (play), Aralan (study) at Sanayan (training) TuKLASan Center. Its primary aim is to provide care, protective, rehabilitative and preventive programs and services that will respond to the problems concerning streetchildren. With the downpour of responses to ERDA's call in helping TuKLASan Center, its new building was constructed in San Juan, Metro Manila and was named the Ma. Elena Yuchengco House (MEYH).
MEYH serves as a temporary shelter for streetchildren (particularly boys) ages 17 and below. It can accommodate a minimum of 25 to a maximum of 50 children for a period of time. The children are allowed to stay in the center for six months to two years. It targets to house 100 to 150 children each year. Open 24 hours a day, TuKLASan accepts children who go to the center on their own, or through referrals from other institutions. This special project of ERDA was anchored to the Philippine Program for Streetchildren.
